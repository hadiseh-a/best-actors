Alan Rickman

Alan Sidney Patrick Rickman (February 21, 1946 - January 14, 2016) was an English actor and director. His films have grossed over $10 billion worldwide, making him one of the highest-grossing actors of all time. Known for his deep and distinctive voice, Rickman trained at RADA (Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts) in London, joined the Royal Shakespeare Company (RSC) and performed in modern and classical theater productions. In 1985 he played the Vicomte de Valmont in the RSC stage production of Les Liaisons Dangereuses and was nominated for a Tony Award after the film transferred to the West End in 1986 and Broadway in 1987.
